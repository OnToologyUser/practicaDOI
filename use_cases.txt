USE CASES FOR THE OSCAR AWARDS & IMDb ONTOLOGY
================================================================================
Datasets used:
  [D1] Kaggle – The Oscar Award (1927–2026)
       https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/unanimad/the-oscar-award
       File: csv/the_oscar_award.csv
       Key columns: year_film, year_ceremony, ceremony, category, name, film, winner
 
  [D2] IMDb – Non-commercial datasets
       https://datasets.imdbws.com/
       Files: csv/title.basics.tsv, csv/name.basics.tsv
       Key columns (titles): tconst, primaryTitle, originalTitle, startYear, genres
       Key columns (names):  nconst, primaryName, birthYear, deathYear, primaryProfession
 
================================================================================
USE CASE 1 — Query Oscar Winners by Category and Year
================================================================================
 
Goal:
  Users (film enthusiasts, journalists, researchers) want to retrieve award
  winners filtered by a specific category and/or year. This is the most
  fundamental query over the awards data and validates that nominations, wins,
  and ceremony metadata are correctly modelled.
 
Primary dataset: [D1] the_oscar_award.csv
 
Competency Questions:
  CQ1.  Which films were nominated for Best Picture in the 59th ceremony (1987)?
        → uses: film, ceremony, category, winner=False
        Example answer: Children of a Lesser God, Hannah and Her Sisters, The Mission
 
  CQ2.  Who won Best Actor at the 1st Academy Awards ceremony?
        → uses: name, category, winner=True, ceremony=1
        Example answer: Emil Jannings
 
  CQ3.  In which ceremony was the award for Best Picture given to The Godfather?
        → uses: film, category, ceremony, winner=True
        Example answer: 45th ceremony (1973)
 
  CQ4.  What award categories existed in the 59th ceremony?
        → uses: category, ceremony
        Example answer: SHORT FILM (Live Action), ACTOR IN A LEADING ROLE, ...
 
  CQ5.  Which films won at least one Oscar in the year 2001?
        → uses: film, year_film, winner=True
        Example answer: Gladiator, Traffic, Crouching Tiger Hidden Dragon, ...
 
  CQ6.  Who were all the nominees (not just winners) in the BEST PICTURE
        category across ceremonies 80–90?
        → uses: name, film, category, ceremony, winner

USE CASE 2 — Analyse Nomination Counts and Patterns per Film
================================================================================
 
Goal:
  Film industry analysts and award strategists need to understand how many
  nominations each film received across all categories, and which films
  historically accumulated the most nominations. This use case stresses the
  aggregation capabilities of the ontology over the awards dataset.
 
Primary dataset: [D1] the_oscar_award.csv
 
Competency Questions:
  CQ7.  How many total Oscar nominations did Titanic (1997) receive?
        → uses: film, year_film, category  (count of rows where film = "Titanic")
        Example answer: 14
 
  CQ8.  Which ten films have received the most nominations in history?
        → uses: film, category  (GROUP BY film, COUNT)
        Example answer: All About Eve (14), Titanic (14), La La Land (14), ...
 
  CQ9.  Did any film receive nominations in both acting and directing categories
        in the same ceremony?
        → uses: film, category, ceremony
        Example answer: Yes – The Godfather (1972) nominated for Director and actors
 
  CQ10. How many films were nominated but received zero wins in ceremony 95?
        → uses: film, ceremony, winner
        Example answer: requires counting films where all winner=False in ceremony=95
 
  CQ11. In which categories was the film "Everything Everywhere All at Once"
        nominated, and did it win each?
        → uses: film, category, winner
        Example answer: Best Picture ✓, Best Director ✓, Best Actress ✓, ...

USE CASE 3 — Cross-Dataset Film Lookup (Awards + IMDb Metadata)
================================================================================
 
Goal:
  Researchers and data integrators need to enrich Oscar nomination records with
  IMDb film metadata (genre, original title, release year) using the shared film
  identifier (tconst). This use case validates that the ontology correctly links
  both datasets through a common key.
 
Primary datasets: [D1] the_oscar_award.csv  +  [D2] title.basics.tsv
 
Competency Questions:
  CQ12. What is the IMDb identifier (tconst) of the film associated with a
        given Oscar nomination record?
        → uses: film [D1] linked to tconst [D2]
        Example answer: Platoon (1986) → tt0091763
 
  CQ13. What genre(s) does IMDb assign to the Best Picture winner of
        ceremony 59?
        → uses: film [D1], winner=True, category="BEST PICTURE" [D1]
                joined to genres [D2] via tconst
        Example answer: Platoon → Drama, War
 
  CQ14. What is the original (non-English) title of an Oscar-nominated film
        whose primary IMDb title differs from the name recorded in the
        awards dataset?
        → uses: film [D1] joined to originalTitle [D2]
        Example answer: "Amour" → originalTitle = "Amour" (FR)
 
  CQ15. Which Oscar-nominated films were released in a different year than the
        year recorded in the awards dataset?
        → uses: year_film [D1] vs startYear [D2]
        (validates date alignment between datasets)
 
  CQ16. For a given tconst (e.g. tt0099674), what Oscar nominations does the
        corresponding film have?
        → uses: tconst [D2] joined to film [D1] via shared key
        Example answer: tt0099674 = "Goodfellas" → nominated for Best Picture (1991)

USE CASE 4 — Cross-Dataset Person Lookup (Awards + IMDb Profiles)
================================================================================
 
Goal:
  Casting directors, researchers, and journalists need to correlate Oscar
  nomination data with biographical and professional information about the
  nominated individuals stored in the IMDb names dataset. This validates the
  person-level linking between both datasets through the nconst identifier.
 
Primary datasets: [D1] the_oscar_award.csv  +  [D2] name.basics.tsv
 
Competency Questions:
  CQ17. What is the IMDb identifier (nconst) of a person nominated for an Oscar?
        → uses: name [D1] joined to nconst [D2]
        Example answer: Meryl Streep → nm0000658
 
  CQ18. What is the primary profession listed in IMDb for a given Oscar nominee?
        → uses: name [D1] joined to primaryProfession [D2] via nconst
        Example answer: nm0000338 → "actress, soundtrack, producer"
 
  CQ19. Which Oscar nominees in the ACTRESS IN A LEADING ROLE category were
        born before 1940 according to IMDb?
        → uses: name, category [D1] joined to birthYear [D2]
        Example answer: Katharine Hepburn (born 1907), Bette Davis (born 1908)
 
  CQ20. Are there individuals who appear in the Oscar dataset under a name
        different from their primaryName in IMDb?
        → uses: name [D1] vs primaryName [D2]  (validates name normalisation)
 
  CQ21. Which Oscar winners are recorded in IMDb as having deceased before
        receiving their award?
        → uses: winner=True, year_ceremony [D1] vs deathYear [D2]
        (edge case / data quality check)

USE CASE 5 — Historical Trends in Oscar Categories
================================================================================
 
Goal:
  Film historians and ontology engineers need to understand how award categories
  have evolved across ceremonies: when categories were introduced, renamed, or
  discontinued. This use case validates temporal modelling of category metadata.
 
Primary dataset: [D1] the_oscar_award.csv
 
Competency Questions:
  CQ22. What is the canonical (standardised) name of the category historically
        known as "MAKEUP"?
        → uses: category [D1]
        Example answer: MAKEUP AND HAIRSTYLING
 
  CQ23. In which ceremony was the category INTERNATIONAL FEATURE FILM first
        awarded?
        → uses: category, ceremony  (MIN ceremony for that category)
        Example answer: 16th ceremony (1944), formerly FOREIGN LANGUAGE FILM
 
  CQ24. Which categories appear in ceremony 1 but not in ceremony 95?
        → uses: category, ceremony  (SET DIFFERENCE across two ceremony values)
 
  CQ25. How many distinct award categories have existed across all ceremonies?
        → uses: category  (COUNT DISTINCT)
        Example answer: approx. 120+ distinct category strings over the full history
